Matthis Grunsky
paper stereo paint browser

Matthis Grunsky is an artist and educator whose work moves between painting, drawing, artist books, experimental robotics, and browser-based artworks. His work uses repeated motifs, simple rules, and iterative processes to build images that bridge studio materiality with computational structure.

Across studio and digital projects, Matthis focuses on perception by treating drawing as a temporal event. The frequent incorporation of stereoscopic images encourages active participant and links visual depth to iterative computational logic.

Browser works extend his approach through recursive interfaces, cellular automata, and interactive motion systems. These works combine visitor input with autonomous behaviors, producing outcomes that shift from one encounter to the next.
